Introduction to VLSI

VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) is the process of creating integrated circuits by combining thousands or millions of transistors onto a single chip. It’s a cornerstone of modern electronics—enabling compact, powerful, and energy-efficient devices. Explore the stages that translate a high-level specification into a physical silicon layout.

VLSI Design Flow

1. Specifications 2. Architecture Design 3. RTL Design (Verilog/VHDL) 4. Functional Verification 5. Synthesis 6. Design for Test (DFT) 7. Floorplanning 8. Placement 9. Clock Tree Synthesis 10. Routing 11. Physical Verification 12. Timing 13. Sign-off 14. Tape-out
